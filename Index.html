How to Use the Anti-Hope Machine

Step 1 — Enter Your Real Numbers (Left Panel)

Fill in Overall Trading Stats from your broker. These power the baseline for your daily session grades. Leave blank if you're just exploring.

Step 2 — Set Your Strategy (Centre Panel)

Two collapsible groups per tab:

  • Core Settings — the essentials: profit target, stop loss, win rate, trades per day, share goal.
  • Realistic Conditions — costs and chaos: spread, slippage, commission, bad days, discipline. These are what separate paper performance from live reality.

Each slider has a toggle switch. Turn a variable off and it has zero effect on the simulation.

Step 3 — Simulate

Hit ▶ SIMULATE. The sim runs until you reach your share goal (or 5,000 days). You get an equity curve, a day-by-day table, and a verdict box overlaid on the chart: Viable, Marginal, or Failing.

Step 4 — Compare Strategies

Use the Strategy B and Strategy C tabs to test different setups side by side. The left and right panels always reflect the active tab.

Step 5 — Log Your Sessions

After each session, enter your results in Today's Session. After a few days you'll unlock a grade (S to F) comparing today against your running average. Honest, not flattering.

Step 6 — Export

The ⬇ XLSX button exports all settings, edge diagnostics, simulation summary, and the full day table as a CSV that opens in Excel.

Key Terms

  • Tick — smallest price move. US stocks: $0.01 per share.
  • R:R — Reward:Risk. 1.67:1 means you win 1.67 ticks for every tick risked.
  • Edge — expected profit per trade after all costs. Positive = system works.
  • Friction — total cost per trade: commission + slippage + spread.
  • Breakeven trade — closed at entry. Zero P&L before costs.
